# Social Sciences Syllabus

> Systematic study of human society, behavior, and institutions.

Back to [[academic-taxonomy|Universal Taxonomy]] | [[knowledge-index|Knowledge Index]]

---

## 8. Psychology

> The scientific study of mind and behavior — from neural mechanisms to social influence.

### Core Subareas
- [[courses/cognitive-psychology|Cognitive Psychology]] — Perception, attention, memory, language processing, decision-making, problem-solving
- [[courses/clinical-psychology|Clinical Psychology]] — Psychopathology (DSM-5), anxiety/mood disorders, psychotherapy (CBT, psychodynamic), assessment
- [[courses/developmental-psychology|Developmental Psychology]] — Piaget stages, Vygotsky ZPD, attachment (Bowlby), lifespan development
- [[courses/social-psychology|Social Psychology]] — Attribution, conformity (Asch), obedience (Milgram), group dynamics, prejudice, persuasion
- [[courses/behavioral-neuroscience|Behavioral Neuroscience]] — Brain-behavior, neurotransmitters, psychopharmacology, reward circuits, neuroplasticity

### Intersections
- Neuropsychology — Brain lesions, cognitive deficits, rehabilitation, neuroimaging
- Evolutionary Psychology — Adaptive problems, mating strategies, parental investment, cognitive modules
- Educational Psychology — Learning theories, motivation, instructional design, metacognition
- Forensic Psychology — Criminal profiling, eyewitness testimony, competency, risk assessment

### Key References
- Kandel et al., *Principles of Neural Science*
- Nolen-Hoeksema et al., *Abnormal Psychology*
- Myers & DeWall, *Psychology* (survey text)
- Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow*

## 9. Sociology

> The study of social life, social change, and the social causes and consequences of human behavior.

### Core Subareas
- [[courses/sociology|Sociology]] — Social stratification, institutions, organizations, culture, socialization, inequality
- Demography — Population structure, fertility/mortality, migration, demographic transition
- [[courses/criminology|Criminology]] — Crime theories (strain, labeling, rational choice), criminal justice, penology
- Urban & Rural Sociology — Urbanization, spatial inequality, gentrification, community studies

### Intersections
- Political Sociology — Power, state, social movements, revolution, political culture
- Economic Sociology — Markets as social structures, embeddedness (Granovetter), institutional economics

### Key References
- Giddens, *Sociology*
- Weber, *Economy and Society*
- Durkheim, *The Rules of Sociological Method*
- Bourdieu, *Distinction*

---

## 10. Economics

> The study of how societies allocate scarce resources.

- [[courses/microeconomics|Microeconomics]] — Consumer/producer theory, equilibrium, game theory, mechanism design, behavioral
- [[courses/macroeconomics|Macroeconomics]] — GDP, inflation, monetary/fiscal policy, IS-LM, growth theory (Solow)
- [[courses/econometrics|Econometrics]] — OLS, IV, panel data, diff-in-diff, RDD, GMM
- Behavioral Economics — Prospect theory, framing, bounded rationality, nudge theory
- Labor Economics — Wage determination, human capital, discrimination, search models
- Development Economics — Poverty traps, microfinance, RCTs, institutions
- International Economics — Trade theory, exchange rates, balance of payments

### Key References
- Mas-Colell, Whinston & Green, *Microeconomic Theory*
- Blanchard, *Macroeconomics*
- Angrist & Pischke, *Mostly Harmless Econometrics*
- Acemoglu & Robinson, *Why Nations Fail*

---

## 11. Political Science

> The study of politics, power, government, and public policies.

- [[courses/political-science|Comparative Politics]] — Regime types, democratization, electoral systems, parties, federalism
- [[courses/international-relations|International Relations]] — Realism, liberalism, constructivism, security studies, international organizations
- [[courses/political-theory|Political Theory]] — Social contract, justice (Rawls), liberty (Mill, Berlin), republicanism
- Public Policy & Administration — Policy process, implementation, evaluation, bureaucracy, governance

### Key References
- Dahl, *Who Governs?*
- Waltz, *Theory of International Politics*
- Rawls, *A Theory of Justice*
- Fukuyama, *The Origins of Political Order*

---

## 12. Anthropology & Archaeology

> The study of humans — past and present — in all their biological, cultural, and linguistic diversity.

- [[courses/cultural-anthropology|Cultural Anthropology]] — Ethnography, kinship, religion, ritual, economic anthropology, globalization
- Physical/Biological Anthropology — Human evolution, primatology, forensic anthropology, variation
- Linguistic Anthropology — Language and culture, Sapir-Whorf, discourse analysis, endangered languages
- [[courses/archaeology|Archaeology]] — Excavation methods, dating techniques, material culture, settlement patterns

### Key References
- Kottak, *Cultural Anthropology*
- Ember & Ember, *Anthropology*
- Renfrew & Bahn, *Archaeology: Theories, Methods, Practice*
