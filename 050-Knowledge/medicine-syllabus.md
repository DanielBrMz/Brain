# Medicine & Health Sciences Syllabus

> Applied sciences of human health — from molecular mechanisms to public health policy.

Back to [[academic-taxonomy|Universal Taxonomy]] | [[knowledge-index|Knowledge Index]]

---

## 17. Medicine & Health Sciences

### Foundational Sciences
- [[courses/anatomy|Anatomy]] — Gross anatomy (regional/systemic), histology, embryology, neuroanatomy, surface anatomy, cross-sectional anatomy (CT/MRI correlation)
- [[courses/physiology|Physiology]] — Organ system physiology: cardiovascular ($CO = SV \times HR$), respiratory, renal, endocrine, nervous (see also [[../sciences-syllabus|Sciences Syllabus]])
- [[courses/biochemistry|Biochemistry]] — Enzyme kinetics, metabolism, molecular biology (see also [[../sciences-syllabus|Sciences Syllabus]])

### Pharmacology & Therapeutics
- [[courses/pharmacology|Pharmacology]] — Pharmacokinetics (ADME, half-life $t_{1/2} = 0.693/k_e$), pharmacodynamics (dose-response, $EC_{50}$, therapeutic index), drug classes by system, toxicology, clinical pharmacology

### Disease & Diagnosis
- [[courses/pathology|Pathology]] — General pathology (cell injury, inflammation, neoplasia, hemodynamics), systemic pathology (by organ system), clinical pathology (lab medicine), molecular pathology
- [[courses/epidemiology|Epidemiology]] — Study designs (cohort, case-control, RCT, cross-sectional), measures (incidence, prevalence, RR, OR, NNT), bias/confounding, screening (sensitivity/specificity), outbreak investigation

### Mental Health
- [[courses/psychiatry|Psychiatry]] — Psychiatric disorders (DSM-5/ICD-11: mood, anxiety, psychotic, personality, substance use), psychopharmacology (SSRIs, antipsychotics, mood stabilizers), psychotherapy modalities, forensic psychiatry

### Population Health
- [[courses/public-health|Public Health]] — Health policy, health economics, environmental health, occupational health, global health (WHO, burden of disease), health promotion, social determinants of health, health systems

### Immunology (Clinical)
- [[courses/immunology|Immunology]] — Clinical immunology, autoimmune diseases, immunodeficiency, transplant immunology, tumor immunology, immunotherapy (see also [[../sciences-syllabus|Sciences Syllabus]])
