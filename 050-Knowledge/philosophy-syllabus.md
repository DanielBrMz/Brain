# Philosophy Syllabus

> The study of fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, values, reason, and reality.

Back to [[academic-taxonomy|Universal Taxonomy]] | [[knowledge-index|Knowledge Index]]

---

## 13. Philosophy

### Epistemology (Theory of Knowledge)
- [[courses/epistemology|Epistemology]] — What is knowledge? Justified true belief, Gettier problems, skepticism (Cartesian, Humean), foundationalism vs coherentism, reliabilism, virtue epistemology, social epistemology, epistemic injustice

### Ethics (Moral Philosophy)
- [[courses/ethics|Ethics]] — Metaethics (moral realism, expressivism, error theory), normative ethics (consequentialism/utilitarianism, deontology/Kant, virtue ethics/Aristotle), applied ethics (bioethics, business ethics, environmental ethics, AI ethics)

### Metaphysics
- [[courses/metaphysics|Metaphysics]] — Substance and properties, causation (Hume, counterfactual), personal identity, time (A-theory vs B-theory), modality (possible worlds), free will (compatibilism, libertarianism, hard determinism), philosophy of religion

### Aesthetics
- [[courses/aesthetics|Aesthetics]] — Beauty (objective vs subjective), aesthetic experience, philosophy of art (definition, ontology, interpretation), taste (Hume, Kant), the sublime (Burke, Kant), environmental aesthetics, philosophy of music/film/literature

### Philosophy of Science
- [[courses/philosophy-of-science|Philosophy of Science]] — Demarcation problem, falsifiability (Popper), paradigm shifts (Kuhn), research programmes (Lakatos), underdetermination (Quine-Duhem), scientific realism vs anti-realism, explanation (DN model, causal), philosophy of physics/biology/social science

### Philosophy of Mind
- [[courses/philosophy-of-mind|Philosophy of Mind]] — Mind-body problem, consciousness (hard problem: Chalmers), qualia (Mary's room, inverted spectrum), intentionality (Brentano), functionalism, eliminativism, extended mind (Clark & Chalmers), AI and consciousness, embodied/enactivist cognition

### Political Philosophy
- [[courses/political-philosophy|Political Philosophy]] — Justice (Rawls: veil of ignorance, difference principle), liberty (negative: Berlin, positive: Taylor), rights (natural, human), legitimacy (consent, democratic), civil disobedience (Thoreau, King, Rawls), global justice, distributive justice, republicanism

### Key References (Survey)
- Russell, *The Problems of Philosophy* (introduction)
- Kenny, *A New History of Western Philosophy* (comprehensive)
- Blackburn, *Think* (accessible overview)
- Craig, *Philosophy: A Very Short Introduction*

### Major Figures Timeline
- Ancient: Socrates → Plato → Aristotle
- Medieval: Augustine → Aquinas → Ockham
- Early Modern: Descartes → Spinoza → Leibniz → Locke → Hume → Kant
- 19th Century: Hegel → Mill → Marx → Nietzsche → Kierkegaard
- 20th Century: Russell → Wittgenstein → Heidegger → Sartre → Quine → Rawls → Foucault → Derrida
- Contemporary: Chalmers, Parfit, Nussbaum, Singer, Korsgaard
