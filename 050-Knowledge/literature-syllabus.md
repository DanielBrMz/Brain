## 1. Language & Linguistics

### 1.1 Phonetics & Phonology

#### 1.1.1 Articulatory Phonetics
- Vocal tract anatomy — larynx, pharynx, oral/nasal cavities, articulators
- Consonant classification: place (bilabial through glottal), manner (stop, fricative, affricate, nasal, lateral, approximant), voicing
- Vowel classification: height (close/open), backness (front/back), rounding
- IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet) — complete chart mastery
- Suprasegmentals: stress, tone, intonation, length
- Coarticulation and connected speech processes

#### 1.1.2 Phonology
- Phonemes vs allophones; minimal pairs; complementary distribution
- Distinctive features (Jakobson, Chomsky-Halle): [+voice], [+nasal], [+continuant]
- Phonological rules: assimilation, dissimilation, deletion, epenthesis, metathesis
- Syllable structure: onset, nucleus, coda; sonority hierarchy
- Prosodic hierarchy: mora, syllable, foot, prosodic word, phrase
- Autosegmental phonology — tonal representation
- Optimality Theory (Prince & Smolensky) — constraint ranking, EVAL, GEN

### 1.2 Morphology
- Morpheme types: free vs bound; roots, prefixes, suffixes, infixes, circumfixes
- Inflectional vs derivational morphology
- Compounding, blending, clipping, back-formation, conversion
- Morphological typology: isolating (Mandarin), agglutinative (Turkish), fusional (Latin), polysynthetic (Mohawk)
- Morphophonology — phonological alternations conditioned by morphology
- Productivity and lexicalization; blocking effects

### 1.3 Syntax

#### 1.3.1 Phrase Structure
- Constituency tests: substitution, movement, coordination, ellipsis
- X-bar theory: specifier, head, complement; XP -> Spec X' -> X Comp
- Phrase types: NP, VP, AP, PP, CP, IP/TP
- Argument structure and theta roles (agent, patient, theme, experiencer, goal)
- Subcategorization frames — verbs selecting complements

#### 1.3.2 Transformational & Generative Grammar
- Deep structure vs surface structure (classical model)
- Movement operations: wh-movement, NP-movement (passivization, raising)
- Government and Binding Theory — modules: X-bar, theta, case, binding, bounding, control
- Minimalist Program (Chomsky 1995+) — merge, move, phase theory
- Binding theory: Principle A (anaphors), B (pronouns), C (R-expressions)
- Empty categories: PRO, pro, traces, copies

#### 1.3.3 Alternative Frameworks
- Head-Driven Phrase Structure Grammar (HPSG) — typed feature structures
- Lexical Functional Grammar (LFG) — c-structure and f-structure
- Construction Grammar (Goldberg) — form-meaning pairings
- Dependency grammar — head-dependent relations; projectivity

### 1.4 Semantics
- Sense vs reference (Frege); denotation and connotation
- Lexical relations: synonymy, antonymy, hyponymy, meronymy, polysemy, homonymy
- Compositional semantics — Principle of Compositionality (Frege)
- Predicate logic and lambda calculus in semantics
- Quantifiers: scope ambiguity, donkey sentences, generalized quantifiers
- Tense and aspect — Reichenbachian framework (S, R, E points)
- Modality: epistemic, deontic, dynamic; possible worlds semantics (Kripke)
- Event semantics (Davidson); thematic roles and neo-Davidsonian approaches
- Formal semantics: Montague Grammar; type theory

### 1.5 Pragmatics
- Speech acts (Austin, Searle): locutionary, illocutionary, perlocutionary
- Felicity conditions — preparatory, sincerity, essential conditions
- Grice's Cooperative Principle and maxims: quantity, quality, relation, manner
- Conversational implicature — scalar implicatures; cancellability
- Relevance theory (Sperber & Wilson) — cognitive effort vs effects
- Presupposition — triggers, projection problem, accommodation
- Deixis: person, spatial, temporal, discourse, social
- Politeness theory (Brown & Levinson) — positive/negative face; face-threatening acts
- Discourse analysis — coherence, cohesion, information structure (topic, focus, given/new)

### 1.6 Historical & Comparative Linguistics
- Language families: Indo-European, Sino-Tibetan, Afro-Asiatic, Niger-Congo, Austronesian
- Comparative method — cognates, sound correspondences, proto-language reconstruction
- Grimm's Law, Verner's Law — Germanic sound shifts
- Mechanisms of change: sound change (regular), analogy, grammaticalization, reanalysis
- Pidgins, creoles, language contact phenomena; substrate/superstrate
- Sociolinguistics: dialects, registers, code-switching, language attitudes

### 1.7 Languages

#### 1.7.1 Spanish
- Phonology: 5-vowel system; seseo/distincion; yeismo; aspiration of /s/
- Verbal morphology: indicative, subjunctive, imperative; tense/aspect/mood system
- Preterite vs imperfect: perfective vs imperfective aspect
- Subjunctive triggers: volition, doubt, emotion, negated belief (WEIRDO)
- Ser vs estar — permanent/inherent vs temporary/resultant states
- Dialectal variation: Rioplatense, Mexican, Caribbean, Andean, Peninsular
- Voseo (Argentina, Central America) — vos conjugation patterns
- Register: tuteo vs ustedeo; formal/informal address systems
- Literary Spanish — classical and modern prose conventions

#### 1.7.2 English
- Germanic roots with massive Romance/Latin/Greek lexical borrowing
- Great Vowel Shift — systematic restructuring of long vowels (1400-1700)
- English morphosyntax: SVO order, limited inflection, extensive use of auxiliaries
- Modals and tense-aspect: progressive, perfect, modal perfect
- Varieties: General American, RP, AAVE, Indian English, Singlish
- English orthography — irregular spelling, historical fossilization

#### 1.7.3 Portuguese
- Relationship to Spanish — mutual intelligibility, false cognates
- Brazilian vs European Portuguese: phonological differences (vowel reduction, palatalization)
- Verb system: personal infinitive (unique among Romance languages)
- Future subjunctive (retained, unlike Spanish)
- Nasal vowels and diphthongs
- Pronoun placement: proclisis (Brazil) vs enclisis (Portugal)

#### 1.7.4 French
- Phonology: nasal vowels, uvular /R/, liaison, enchainement, elision
- Extensive silent letters and complex orthography-phonology mapping
- Verb system: passe compose vs imparfait; subjonctif
- ne...pas negation; ne-drop in spoken French
- Register stratification: written formal vs spoken informal
- Francophonie — global French varieties (Quebec, West Africa, Maghreb)

### 1.8 Computational Linguistics & NLP
- Tokenization, stemming, lemmatization; BPE and SentencePiece
- Part-of-speech tagging — HMMs, CRFs, neural taggers
- Syntactic parsing — constituency (CKY), dependency (arc-eager, biaffine)
- Semantic representations: AMR, semantic role labeling, word sense disambiguation
- Word embeddings: Word2Vec (CBOW, Skip-gram), GloVe, FastText
- Contextual embeddings: ELMo, BERT, GPT architectures
- Machine translation: statistical (phrase-based), neural (seq2seq, attention, transformer)
- Large language models — pretraining, fine-tuning, RLHF, prompting strategies
- Evaluation: BLEU, ROUGE, perplexity, human evaluation frameworks
- Ethics in NLP: bias, fairness, toxicity, multilingual equity

## 2. Writing

### 2.1 Academic Writing
- Thesis-driven argumentation — claim, evidence, warrant (Toulmin model)
- Literature review methodology — synthesis vs summary; identifying gaps
- Citation systems: APA 7th, MLA 9th, Chicago/Turabian, IEEE
- Structure: IMRaD for empirical papers; humanities essay conventions
- Hedging and epistemic stance — appropriate tentativeness
- Peer review process — giving and receiving scholarly critique
- Research proposal writing — problem statement, methodology, significance
- Grant writing — specific aims, significance, innovation, approach

### 2.2 Technical Writing
- Documentation types: API docs, user guides, tutorials, reference manuals
- Information architecture — task-based organization, progressive disclosure
- Style guides: Microsoft, Google developer docs, Apple
- Docs-as-code approach — Markdown, static site generators, version control
- README conventions — project description, installation, usage, contribution
- Diagramming: UML, sequence diagrams, architecture diagrams
- DITA (Darwin Information Typing Architecture) — topic-based authoring

### 2.3 Creative Writing

#### 2.3.1 Fiction Craft
- Narrative point of view: first, second, third limited, third omniscient, unreliable narrator
- Plot structures: Freytag's pyramid, three-act, hero's journey (Campbell), Harmon's story circle
- Character development: flat/round (Forster), static/dynamic; indirect characterization
- Setting and world-building — concrete sensory detail; defamiliarization
- Dialogue: subtext, dialect representation, attributions, pacing with dialogue
- Show vs tell — scene vs summary; objective correlative (Eliot)
- Revision: macro (structure, pacing) vs micro (sentence, word choice)

#### 2.3.2 Poetry Craft
- Meter: iambic, trochaic, anapestic, dactylic, spondaic; scansion notation
- Fixed forms: sonnet (Petrarchan, Shakespearean), villanelle, sestina, ghazal, haiku, terza rima
- Free verse — Whitman through contemporary; line break as meaning unit
- Sound devices: alliteration, assonance, consonance, internal rhyme, enjambment
- Imagery and figuration: metaphor, simile, metonymy, synecdoche, personification
- The image (Pound's imagism); objective correlative; deep image
- Prose poetry and hybrid forms

#### 2.3.3 Creative Nonfiction
- Literary journalism (Wolfe, Didion, Herr) — immersion, scene, dialogue
- Personal essay and memoir — vulnerability, reflection, discovery
- Lyric essay — fragmentation, association, white space as structure
- Travel writing — beyond the exotic; ethical representation
- Nature writing — Thoreau through Kimmerer; ecocritical awareness

### 2.4 Rhetoric & Persuasion
- Classical rhetoric: inventio, dispositio, elocutio, memoria, actio
- Aristotle's appeals: ethos (credibility), pathos (emotion), logos (logic)
- Kairos — the opportune moment; situational awareness
- Rhetorical modes: narration, description, exposition, argumentation
- Logical fallacies — ad hominem, straw man, false dichotomy, appeal to authority, etc.
- Figures of speech: anaphora, chiasmus, antithesis, parallelism, anadiplosis
- Modern rhetoric: Burke's identification; Perelman's new rhetoric
- Digital rhetoric — multimodal composition, visual argument

## 3. Literature

### 3.1 Literary Theory & Criticism

#### 3.1.1 Formalism & Structuralism
- Russian Formalism (Shklovsky) — defamiliarization (ostranenie), the literary device
- New Criticism — close reading, intentional fallacy, affective fallacy; the well-wrought urn
- Structuralism (Saussure, Barthes, Levi-Strauss) — sign = signifier + signified; binary oppositions
- Narratology (Genette): story vs discourse; focalization; analepsis/prolepsis; frequency

#### 3.1.2 Post-Structuralism & Deconstruction
- Derrida — differance, logocentrism, supplementarity; there is nothing outside the text
- Barthes — death of the author; readerly vs writerly texts (S/Z)
- Foucault — discourse, power/knowledge, author function; episteme

#### 3.1.3 Psychoanalytic & Marxist Criticism
- Freud — the uncanny, dream-work, Oedipus complex in literature
- Lacan — the mirror stage, the symbolic/imaginary/real; desire and lack
- Marxist criticism — base/superstructure; ideology; commodity fetishism
- Lukacs — realism and totality; historical novel
- Jameson — political unconscious; postmodernism as cultural logic of late capitalism
- Eagleton — ideology and literary form

#### 3.1.4 Postcolonial, Feminist & Other Approaches
- Postcolonial theory: Said (Orientalism), Spivak (subaltern), Bhabha (hybridity, mimicry, third space)
- Feminist criticism: Woolf, Beauvoir, Showalter (gynocriticism), Cixous (ecriture feminine)
- Gender and queer theory: Butler (performativity), Sedgwick (homosocial desire)
- Ecocriticism — nature writing; environmental humanities
- Reader-response: Iser (implied reader), Fish (interpretive communities)
- New Historicism (Greenblatt) — text and context; cultural poetics

### 3.2 Genres & Forms

#### 3.2.1 The Novel
- Origins: Cervantes (*Don Quixote*), Defoe, Richardson, Fielding
- Bildungsroman — *Wilhelm Meister*, *Jane Eyre*, *Portrait of the Artist*
- The realist novel: Balzac, Dickens, Tolstoy, Eliot, Flaubert
- Modernist novel: stream of consciousness (Woolf, Joyce), fragmentation, unreliable narration
- Postmodern novel: metafiction, pastiche, historiographic metafiction
- The novella — form, compression, unity of effect

#### 3.2.2 Short Fiction
- Poe — unity of effect theory; single-sitting reading
- Chekhov — the modern short story; epiphanic structure; subtext
- Hemingway — iceberg theory; omission as technique
- Borges — ficciones as philosophical inquiry; labyrinth as form
- Flash fiction and microfiction — Lydia Davis, sudden fiction

#### 3.2.3 Drama
- Greek tragedy: Aristotle's *Poetics* — mimesis, catharsis, hamartia, peripeteia, anagnorisis
- Shakespeare — blank verse, soliloquy, five-act structure, comedy/tragedy/history/romance
- Modern drama: Ibsen (realism), Chekhov (subtext), Brecht (epic theater, Verfremdungseffekt)
- Absurdist theater: Beckett (*Waiting for Godot*), Ionesco, Pinter
- Contemporary: August Wilson cycle, Sarah Kane, Suzan-Lori Parks

#### 3.2.4 Poetry Traditions
- Epic poetry: *Iliad*, *Odyssey*, *Aeneid*, *Divine Comedy*, *Paradise Lost*
- Lyric poetry: Sappho through confessional (Plath, Lowell, Sexton)
- Metaphysical poets (Donne, Herbert) — conceits, wit, intellectual argument
- Romantic poetry: Wordsworth, Keats, Shelley, Blake — imagination, nature, the sublime
- Modernist poetry: Eliot (*The Waste Land*), Pound (cantos), Stevens, Williams
- Language poetry, post-confessional, documentary poetics

### 3.3 World Literature

#### 3.3.1 Latin American Literature
- **Jorge Luis Borges** — *Ficciones*, *El Aleph*; the labyrinth, infinite library, the double; metaphysical detective fiction; the essay-as-fiction
- **Gabriel Garcia Marquez** — *Cien anos de soledad*, *El amor en los tiempos del colera*; magical realism; Macondo as mythic space; circular time; solitude as theme
- **Juan Rulfo** — *Pedro Paramo*, *El llano en llamas*; fragmented narrative; voices of the dead; Comala as ghost town; Mexican landscape; sparse, lyrical prose
- **Octavio Paz** — *El laberinto de la soledad*, *Piedra de sol*; Mexican identity; the mask; time as cyclical; poetry and philosophy intertwined
- **Julio Cortazar** — *Rayuela*, *Bestiario*, *Las armas secretas*; the anti-novel; reader as active participant; the fantastic in the everyday; jazz and literature; Paris/Buenos Aires duality
- **Carlos Fuentes** — *La muerte de Artemio Cruz*, *Terra Nostra*; Mexican Revolution and memory; fragmented consciousness; history as fiction; baroque narrative; national identity
- **Other essential figures:** Pablo Neruda (lyric/political poetry, *Canto General*), Cesar Vallejo (avant-garde poetry, *Trilce*), Roberto Bolano (*2666*, *Los detectives salvajes*), Clarice Lispector (Brazilian modernism, interior monologue), Machado de Assis (*Dom Casmurro*, unreliable narration)
- **Boom and Post-Boom:** the 1960s explosion; Donoso, Vargas Llosa, Cabrera Infante; Post-Boom: McOndo, Crack movement

#### 3.3.2 European Literature
- Ancient: Homer, Virgil, Ovid, Greek tragedy (Sophocles, Euripides, Aeschylus)
- Medieval: Dante (*Commedia*), Chaucer, troubadour lyric, Arthurian romance
- Renaissance: Shakespeare, Cervantes, Montaigne, Petrarch
- 19th century: Dostoevsky, Tolstoy, Flaubert, Dickens, Baudelaire, Kafka
- Modernism: Proust, Joyce, Woolf, Mann, Musil, Pessoa
- Contemporary: Calvino, Saramago, Sebald, Knausgaard, Ferrante

#### 3.3.3 Other World Traditions
- Arabic: pre-Islamic poetry (mu'allaqat), *One Thousand and One Nights*, Mahfouz, Darwish
- African: Achebe (*Things Fall Apart*), Ngugi, Soyinka, Adichie, Coetzee
- South Asian: Tagore, Rushdie, Arundhati Roy, Faiz Ahmed Faiz
- East Asian: classical Chinese poetry (Li Bai, Du Fu), *Tale of Genji* (Murasaki), Mishima, Murakami
- North American: Whitman, Dickinson, Faulkner, Morrison, Pynchon

## 4. Music & Song

### 4.1 Music Theory

#### 4.1.1 Fundamentals
- Pitch and frequency; octave equivalence; equal temperament (A4 = 440 Hz)
- Intervals: unison through octave; quality (major, minor, perfect, augmented, diminished)
- Scales: major, natural/harmonic/melodic minor, pentatonic, blues, modes (Ionian through Locrian)
- Key signatures and the circle of fifths
- Rhythm: beat, meter (simple/compound; duple/triple/quadruple), syncopation, polyrhythm
- Time signatures: 4/4, 3/4, 6/8, 5/4, 7/8; additive meters

#### 4.1.2 Harmony
- Triads: major, minor, diminished, augmented; inversions (root, first, second)
- Seventh chords: Maj7, m7, dom7, m7b5 (half-dim), dim7; extended chords (9, 11, 13)
- Roman numeral analysis: I-IV-V-I, ii-V-I, vi-IV-I-V
- Harmonic function: tonic (I, vi), subdominant (IV, ii), dominant (V, vii)
- Voice leading — smooth motion; avoid parallel fifths/octaves; resolution of tendency tones
- Secondary dominants (V/V, V/vi) and borrowed chords (modal mixture)
- Modulation techniques: pivot chord, chromatic, direct, enharmonic
- Jazz harmony: tritone substitution, backdoor progression, Coltrane changes

#### 4.1.3 Counterpoint & Form
- Species counterpoint (Fux): first through fifth species
- Fugue: subject, answer, countersubject, episodes, stretto
- Musical forms: binary (AB), ternary (ABA), rondo (ABACA), sonata-allegro
- Song form: verse-chorus, AABA (32-bar), 12-bar blues
- Through-composed vs strophic; theme and variations

### 4.2 Songwriting

#### 4.2.1 Lyrics & Prosody
- Lyric structure: verse, pre-chorus, chorus, bridge, outro; hook placement
- Rhyme schemes: ABAB, AABB, ABCB; near rhyme, internal rhyme
- Prosody — matching melodic stress to lyrical stress; natural speech rhythm
- Imagery and specificity — concrete detail over abstraction
- Point of view in songs: first person, second person, third person narrative
- Title and hook theory — positional, rhythmic, melodic hooks

#### 4.2.2 Melody & Composition
- Melodic contour: conjunct vs disjunct motion; climax placement
- Range and tessitura — writing for voice type
- Motif development: repetition, sequence, inversion, augmentation, fragmentation
- Tension and release through melodic-harmonic interaction
- Pentatonic melodies and blues notes — accessibility and emotion
- Song demos: chord charts, lead sheets, rough recordings

#### 4.2.3 Production Fundamentals
- Arrangement: instrumentation, texture, dynamics, builds
- Song sections as arrangement units — sparse verse, full chorus
- Recording basics: tracking, overdubbing, comping
- Mixing concepts: EQ, compression, reverb, panning, levels
- DAW workflows: Logic Pro, Ableton Live, Pro Tools, Reaper
- Collaboration: co-writing, toplining, track production

### 4.3 Music History

#### 4.3.1 Western Art Music
- Medieval (500-1400): Gregorian chant, organum, ars nova (Machaut)
- Renaissance (1400-1600): polyphony, Palestrina, Josquin, madrigal
- Baroque (1600-1750): Bach, Handel, Vivaldi; basso continuo, fugue, concerto, opera
- Classical (1750-1820): Mozart, Haydn, early Beethoven; sonata form, symphony, string quartet
- Romantic (1820-1900): Beethoven (late), Chopin, Brahms, Wagner, Tchaikovsky; program music, Gesamtkunstwerk
- 20th century: Debussy (impressionism), Stravinsky (neoclassicism), Schoenberg (serialism), Cage (aleatory), minimalism (Reich, Glass, Riley)

#### 4.3.2 Popular Music
- Blues: Delta (Robert Johnson), Chicago (Muddy Waters); 12-bar form, blue notes
- Jazz: New Orleans, swing, bebop (Parker, Gillespie), cool, hard bop, modal (Miles), free (Coltrane, Ornette), fusion
- Rock & roll: roots (Chuck Berry, Little Richard), British Invasion, psychedelic, punk, alternative, indie
- Soul/R&B/Funk: Motown, Stax, James Brown, Prince, neo-soul
- Hip-hop: DJing, MCing, sampling, production evolution; regional styles
- Electronic: Kraftwerk, house, techno, ambient, IDM, EDM
- Latin music: son cubano, salsa, bossa nova, MPB, cumbia, reggaeton, Latin trap

#### 4.3.3 Music & Culture
- Music as social commentary — protest songs, political music
- Music and identity: race, gender, class, nationality
- The music industry: labels, publishing, streaming economics, royalties
- Copyright and sampling — legal frameworks, fair use
- Live performance and ritual — concert, festival, liturgical traditions
- Technology and music: recording, radio, MTV, streaming, AI generation
