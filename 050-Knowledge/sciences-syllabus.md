# I. Physics

## 1. Classical Mechanics

### 1.1 Newtonian Mechanics
- Newton's three laws of motion
- Inertial and non-inertial reference frames
- Forces: gravity, friction (static/kinetic), normal, tension, drag
- Projectile motion and kinematics in 1D/2D/3D
- Work, kinetic energy, potential energy, work-energy theorem
- Conservation of energy, momentum, angular momentum
- Center of mass and reduced mass problems
- Circular motion, centripetal and centrifugal (pseudo) forces
- Non-inertial frames: Coriolis force, Foucault pendulum

### 1.2 Lagrangian Mechanics
- Generalized coordinates and constraints (holonomic, non-holonomic)
- Principle of least action (Hamilton's principle)
- Euler-Lagrange equations of motion
- Cyclic coordinates and conserved quantities
- Noether's theorem — symmetry and conservation laws
- Calculus of variations
- Applications: double pendulum, bead on a wire, Atwood machine

### 1.3 Hamiltonian Mechanics
- Legendre transform from Lagrangian to Hamiltonian
- Hamilton's canonical equations
- Phase space and Liouville's theorem
- Canonical transformations and generating functions
- Poisson brackets and their relation to quantum commutators
- Hamilton-Jacobi theory
- Action-angle variables and integrable systems

### 1.4 Rigid Body Dynamics
- Moment of inertia tensor, principal axes
- Euler's equations for rigid body rotation
- Euler angles and rotation matrices
- Precession, nutation, and gyroscopic motion
- Free symmetric and asymmetric tops

### 1.5 Oscillations and Waves
- Simple harmonic oscillator — undamped, damped, driven
- Resonance and quality factor (Q-factor)
- Coupled oscillators, normal modes
- Continuous systems: wave equation on a string
- Standing waves, boundary conditions
- Fourier analysis of periodic motion

### 1.6 Central Force Problems
- Effective potential and orbit classification
- Kepler's laws and orbital mechanics
- Scattering theory: Rutherford scattering, cross-sections
- Two-body problem reduction

## 2. Electromagnetism

### 2.1 Electrostatics
- Coulomb's law and superposition principle
- Electric field and electric potential
- Gauss's law and applications (spherical, cylindrical, planar symmetry)
- Conductors, capacitance, and dielectrics
- Boundary conditions at interfaces
- Laplace and Poisson equations
- Method of images, separation of variables, multipole expansion

### 2.2 Magnetostatics
- Biot-Savart law and Ampere's law
- Magnetic vector potential
- Magnetic dipoles, magnetization
- Magnetic materials: diamagnetic, paramagnetic, ferromagnetic
- Boundary conditions for magnetic fields

### 2.3 Electrodynamics
- Faraday's law of induction and Lenz's law
- Maxwell's displacement current
- Maxwell's equations in differential and integral form
- Electromagnetic energy: Poynting vector, energy density
- Maxwell stress tensor
- Electromagnetic momentum and radiation pressure

### 2.4 Electromagnetic Waves
- Wave equation derivation from Maxwell's equations
- Plane waves: polarization (linear, circular, elliptical)
- Reflection, refraction, Fresnel equations
- Waveguides and cavity resonators
- Electromagnetic spectrum classification

### 2.5 Radiation
- Retarded potentials and Lienard-Wiechert potentials
- Electric dipole radiation, Larmor formula
- Radiation from accelerating charges
- Antenna theory basics
- Synchrotron and Bremsstrahlung radiation

### 2.6 Special Relativity
- Lorentz transformations and spacetime intervals
- Four-vectors: position, momentum, current density
- Relativistic energy-momentum relation (E^2 = p^2c^2 + m^2c^4)
- Electromagnetic field tensor F^{\mu\nu}
- Covariant formulation of Maxwell's equations
- Relativistic Doppler effect and aberration
- Thomas precession

## 3. Thermodynamics & Statistical Mechanics

### 3.1 Laws of Thermodynamics
- **Zeroth Law** — thermal equilibrium and temperature definition
- **First Law** — internal energy, heat, work (dU = dQ - dW)
- **Second Law** — entropy, Clausius/Kelvin-Planck statements, Carnot theorem
- **Third Law** — Nernst theorem, absolute zero unattainability

### 3.2 Thermodynamic Potentials
- Internal energy U, enthalpy H, Helmholtz free energy F, Gibbs free energy G
- Maxwell relations and their derivations
- Legendre transforms between potentials
- Chemical potential and thermodynamic equilibrium
- Clausius-Clapeyron equation

### 3.3 Statistical Mechanics Foundations
- Microstates, macrostates, and ergodic hypothesis
- Boltzmann entropy: S = k_B ln(Omega)
- Equipartition theorem and its breakdown

### 3.4 Ensembles
- Microcanonical ensemble (NVE) — isolated systems
- Canonical ensemble (NVT) — partition function Z, free energy connection
- Grand canonical ensemble (muVT) — grand partition function, chemical potential
- Fluctuations and equivalence of ensembles in thermodynamic limit

### 3.5 Quantum Statistical Mechanics
- Identical particles: bosons and fermions
- Fermi-Dirac distribution — electron gas, Fermi energy, Sommerfeld expansion
- Bose-Einstein distribution — photon gas, Planck distribution, phonons
- Bose-Einstein condensation
- Density of states in various dimensions

### 3.6 Phase Transitions
- First-order vs continuous (second-order) transitions
- Order parameters and spontaneous symmetry breaking
- Landau theory of phase transitions
- Critical phenomena: critical exponents, universality classes
- Ising model (1D exact, 2D Onsager solution outline, mean field)
- Renormalization group concepts

### 3.7 Kinetic Theory
- Maxwell-Boltzmann velocity distribution
- Transport phenomena: viscosity, thermal conductivity, diffusion
- Boltzmann transport equation
- H-theorem and approach to equilibrium
- Mean free path and collision rates

## 4. Quantum Mechanics

### 4.1 Mathematical Formalism
- Hilbert space, state vectors, Dirac notation
- Operators: Hermitian, unitary, projection operators
- Eigenvalue problems and spectral theorem
- Commutation relations and uncertainty principle
- Postulates of quantum mechanics
- Time evolution: Schrodinger equation, time-evolution operator
- Schrodinger, Heisenberg, and interaction pictures

### 4.2 One-Dimensional Problems
- Infinite and finite square well
- Harmonic oscillator — ladder operator (algebraic) method
- Delta-function potential
- Free particle and wave packets
- Tunneling: transmission/reflection coefficients, WKB approximation
- Scattering in 1D: transfer matrix method

### 4.3 Angular Momentum
- Orbital angular momentum: L^2, L_z, spherical harmonics Y_l^m
- Spin angular momentum: Pauli matrices, spinors
- Addition of angular momentum: Clebsch-Gordan coefficients
- Wigner-Eckart theorem

### 4.4 Three-Dimensional Problems
- Central potential: radial equation, effective potential
- Hydrogen atom: exact solution, quantum numbers (n, l, m, m_s)
- Degeneracy and symmetry of hydrogen
- 3D harmonic oscillator

### 4.5 Approximation Methods
- Time-independent perturbation theory (non-degenerate and degenerate)
- Fine structure of hydrogen: relativistic correction, spin-orbit coupling, Darwin term
- Stark effect, Zeeman effect (weak and strong field)
- Variational method and ground state bounds
- WKB approximation and connection formulas
- Time-dependent perturbation theory: Fermi's golden rule
- Adiabatic and sudden approximations

### 4.6 Identical Particles
- Symmetric and antisymmetric wave functions
- Pauli exclusion principle
- Exchange interaction and Slater determinants
- Helium atom ground state (perturbative and variational)

### 4.7 Quantum Entanglement and Information
- Bell states and EPR paradox
- Bell's theorem and Bell inequalities (CHSH)
- Quantum teleportation protocol
- Density matrix formalism — pure and mixed states
- Decoherence and open quantum systems
- Quantum computing basics: qubits, gates, no-cloning theorem

### 4.8 Scattering Theory
- Born approximation
- Partial wave analysis
- Optical theorem
- Phase shifts and cross-sections

## 5. Condensed Matter Physics

### 5.1 Crystal Structure
- Bravais lattices and unit cells (14 lattice types)
- Reciprocal lattice, Brillouin zones
- X-ray diffraction: Bragg's law, structure factor
- Point groups and space groups

### 5.2 Electronic Band Theory
- Nearly-free electron model and Bloch's theorem
- Tight-binding model
- Band gaps and energy bands
- Metals, semiconductors, insulators classification
- Effective mass approximation
- Semiconductor physics: doping, p-n junctions, carrier statistics

### 5.3 Phonons and Lattice Dynamics
- Monatomic and diatomic chain models
- Acoustic and optical phonon branches
- Debye and Einstein models for heat capacity
- Phonon dispersion relations
- Anharmonic effects and thermal expansion

### 5.4 Superconductivity
- Meissner effect, Type I and Type II superconductors
- London equations and penetration depth
- BCS theory: Cooper pairing, energy gap
- Ginzburg-Landau theory and order parameter
- Josephson effect: DC and AC
- High-temperature superconductors (cuprates, iron-based)

### 5.5 Magnetism
- Diamagnetism, paramagnetism (Curie law)
- Ferromagnetism: exchange interaction, Heisenberg model, Curie-Weiss law
- Antiferromagnetism and ferrimagnetism
- Magnetic domains, hysteresis
- Spin waves (magnons)
- Giant magnetoresistance (GMR) and spintronics

### 5.6 Disordered Systems
- Anderson localization
- Amorphous solids and glasses
- Percolation theory
- Topological insulators (overview)

## 6. Particle Physics & Quantum Field Theory

### 6.1 The Standard Model
- Fermions: quarks (6 flavors, 3 generations) and leptons
- Gauge bosons: photon, W+/W-/Z, gluons
- Higgs boson and electroweak symmetry breaking
- Fundamental interactions: electromagnetic, weak, strong, gravitational
- Conservation laws: charge, lepton number, baryon number, flavor (approximate)
- CKM matrix and quark mixing
- CP violation

### 6.2 Quantum Electrodynamics (QED)
- Dirac equation and spinors
- Feynman diagrams and Feynman rules for QED
- Tree-level processes: electron-electron, electron-positron scattering
- Radiative corrections: self-energy, vertex corrections, vacuum polarization
- Renormalization: UV divergences, counterterms, running coupling
- Anomalous magnetic moment of the electron (g-2)
- Lamb shift

### 6.3 Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD)
- Color charge (SU(3) gauge symmetry)
- Asymptotic freedom and confinement
- Deep inelastic scattering and parton model
- Jets and hadronization
- Lattice QCD (overview)

### 6.4 Electroweak Theory
- SU(2)_L x U(1)_Y gauge symmetry
- Spontaneous symmetry breaking and Higgs mechanism
- Weak interactions: charged and neutral currents
- Neutrino oscillations and mass

### 6.5 Gauge Theory Foundations
- Local gauge invariance and minimal coupling
- Yang-Mills theories
- Path integrals in QFT
- Symmetry breaking: Goldstone theorem, Higgs mechanism
- Anomalies (chiral anomaly, ABJ anomaly)

### 6.6 Beyond the Standard Model
- Grand unified theories (GUTs)
- Supersymmetry (SUSY) — concepts
- Dark matter candidates (WIMPs, axions)
- Dark energy and cosmological constant problem
- Neutrino masses and seesaw mechanism
- String theory (overview)

## 7. General Relativity & Cosmology

### 7.1 Differential Geometry
- Manifolds, tangent spaces, metric tensor
- Christoffel symbols and covariant derivative
- Parallel transport and geodesics
- Riemann curvature tensor, Ricci tensor, scalar curvature
- Geodesic deviation

### 7.2 Einstein's Field Equations
- Equivalence principle (weak and strong)
- Einstein tensor and stress-energy tensor
- G_{\mu\nu} + Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = (8piG/c^4) T_{\mu\nu}
- Linearized gravity and gravitational waves
- Weak-field limit and Newtonian approximation
- Post-Newtonian formalism

### 7.3 Exact Solutions
- Schwarzschild metric: black holes, singularities, event horizon
- Kerr metric: rotating black holes, ergosphere, frame dragging
- Reissner-Nordstrom: charged black holes
- FLRW metric: homogeneous isotropic cosmology

### 7.4 Black Holes
- Penrose diagrams and causal structure
- Hawking radiation and black hole thermodynamics
- Information paradox
- Penrose process and black hole energy extraction
- Gravitational lensing

### 7.5 Gravitational Waves
- Linearized gravity and transverse-traceless gauge
- Quadrupole formula for gravitational wave emission
- LIGO/Virgo detection: strain measurement, interferometry
- Binary inspiral, merger, ringdown
- Sources: compact binaries, neutron star mergers, stochastic background

### 7.6 Cosmology
- Friedmann equations and expansion history
- Big Bang nucleosynthesis
- Cosmic microwave background (CMB): anisotropies, power spectrum
- Inflation: horizon and flatness problems, slow-roll models
- Dark matter evidence: rotation curves, gravitational lensing, CMB
- Dark energy and accelerated expansion
- Large-scale structure formation
- Cosmological perturbation theory

- Eulerian vs Lagrangian descriptions
- Stress tensor in fluids

### 10.2 Governing Equations
- Conservation of mass: continuity equation

- Validation and verification

# III. Biology

## 1. Cell Biology

### 1.1 Cell Structure
- Prokaryotic vs eukaryotic cells
- Organelles: nucleus, mitochondria, ER (rough/smooth), Golgi, lysosomes, peroxisomes
- Cytoskeleton: microtubules, actin filaments, intermediate filaments
- Cell membrane: fluid mosaic model, lipid rafts
- Extracellular matrix: collagen, fibronectin, integrins

### 1.2 Membrane Transport
- Passive: simple diffusion, facilitated diffusion, osmosis
- Active transport: Na+/K+ ATPase, Ca2+ ATPase
- Vesicular transport: endocytosis, exocytosis, phagocytosis
- Ion channels: voltage-gated, ligand-gated, mechanosensitive

### 1.3 Cell Signaling
- Signal transduction pathways: GPCR, receptor tyrosine kinases
- Second messengers: cAMP, IP3, DAG, Ca2+
- MAPK/ERK pathway, PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway
- Wnt, Notch, Hedgehog signaling (critical for development)
- Apoptosis: intrinsic (mitochondrial) and extrinsic pathways

### 1.4 Cell Cycle and Division
- Cell cycle phases: G1, S, G2, M
- Cyclin-CDK complexes and checkpoints
- Mitosis: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, telophase
- Meiosis: recombination, independent assortment, reductive division
- Regulation and cancer: oncogenes, tumor suppressors (p53, Rb)

- Initiation, elongation, termination

### 2.4 Gene Regulation
- Epigenetics: DNA methylation, histone modifications (acetylation, methylation)
- Chromatin remodeling complexes
- Transcription factors: activators, repressors, enhancers, silencers
- Non-coding RNAs: miRNA, siRNA, lncRNA, CRISPR
- Operon model (lac, trp)

## 4. Neuroscience (DETAILED — BCH/HMS Research Focus)

> **Note:** This section is expanded given Daniel's work on fetal brain MRI processing at Boston Children's Hospital / Harvard Medical School.

### 4.1 Neuroanatomy

#### 4.1.1 Gross Brain Anatomy
- Cerebral hemispheres: frontal, parietal, temporal, occipital lobes
- Cerebral cortex: six-layered neocortex (molecular, external granular, external pyramidal, internal granular, internal pyramidal, multiform)
- Sulci and gyri: central sulcus, lateral sulcus, cingulate sulcus, calcarine sulcus
- Subcortical structures: basal ganglia (caudate, putamen, globus pallidus), thalamus, hypothalamus
- Limbic system: hippocampus, amygdala, cingulate cortex, fornix
- Brainstem: midbrain, pons, medulla oblongata
- Cerebellum: cerebellar cortex, deep nuclei, peduncles
- Ventricular system: lateral ventricles, third ventricle, cerebral aqueduct, fourth ventricle
- Meninges: dura mater, arachnoid mater, pia mater
- Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF): production (choroid plexus), circulation, absorption (arachnoid granulations)

#### 4.1.2 White Matter Tracts
- Commissural fibers: corpus callosum (genu, body, splenium), anterior commissure
- Association fibers: arcuate fasciculus, superior/inferior longitudinal fasciculus, uncinate fasciculus, cingulum
- Projection fibers: internal capsule (anterior limb, genu, posterior limb), corona radiata
- Relevance to diffusion tractography (DTI) — see Applied Sciences section

#### 4.1.3 Spinal Cord
- Gray matter: dorsal horn (sensory), ventral horn (motor), lateral horn (autonomic)
- White matter: dorsal columns, spinothalamic tract, corticospinal tract
- Spinal nerve organization: 31 pairs, dermatomes

### 4.2 Cellular Neuroscience

#### 4.2.1 Neurons

### 4.4 Synaptic Plasticity

### 4.5 Brain Development (CRITICAL for Fetal Brain MRI Research)

#### 4.5.1 Neural Tube Formation
- Neurulation: neural plate, neural folds, neural tube closure (days 21-28 in humans)
- Neural tube defects: anencephaly (rostral), spina bifida (caudal)
- Primary vs secondary neurulation
- Sonic hedgehog (Shh) patterning: ventral-dorsal axis
- BMP/Wnt signaling: dorsal patterning

#### 4.5.2 Brain Vesicle Formation
- Three-vesicle stage: prosencephalon, mesencephalon, rhombencephalon
- Five-vesicle stage: telencephalon, diencephalon, mesencephalon, metencephalon, myelencephalon
- Cortical progenitor zones: ventricular zone (VZ), subventricular zone (SVZ), outer SVZ (oSVZ)

#### 4.5.3 Neurogenesis and Migration
- Symmetric vs asymmetric division of radial glial progenitors
- Outer radial glia (oRG) — critical for human cortical expansion
- Radial migration: inside-out pattern of cortical layer formation
- Tangential migration: interneuron migration from ganglionic eminences (MGE, LGE, CGE)
- Migration disorders: lissencephaly (LIS1, DCX mutations), periventricular heterotopia
- Timing: peak neurogenesis approximately gestational weeks 10-20

#### 4.5.4 Cortical Folding and Gyrification
- **Primary sulci** formation: gestational weeks 14-26 (central sulcus ~20 weeks, Sylvian fissure ~14 weeks)
- **Secondary sulci**: gestational weeks 26-36
- **Tertiary sulci**: from week 36 through postnatal period
- Gyrification index (GI): ratio of total cortical surface to outer (exposed) surface
- Mechanical theories: differential tangential expansion of cortical layers, axonal tension hypothesis
- Molecular regulation: FGF, Shh, Trnp1, GPR56 mutations (polymicrogyria)
- Abnormal folding patterns:
  - **Lissencephaly** (smooth brain): absent/reduced gyri, LIS1/DCX/TUBA1A mutations
  - **Polymicrogyria**: excessive small irregular gyri, often focal
  - **Pachygyria**: broad flat gyri, reduced sulcation
  - **Schizencephaly**: cleft in cortex lined by gray matter
- **Relevance to fetal MRI:** Sulcal development serves as a biomarker for gestational age assessment and can flag developmental abnormalities

#### 4.5.5 Fetal Brain Development Timeline
- **Weeks 6-10:** Neural tube closure, vesicle formation, early neurogenesis begins
- **Weeks 10-16:** Rapid neurogenesis, cortical plate formation, smooth brain surface
- **Weeks 16-20:** Primary sulci emerge, corpus callosum begins forming
- **Weeks 20-26:** Secondary sulci, rapid cortical expansion, subplate zone prominent
- **Weeks 26-32:** Gyrification accelerates, myelination begins in brainstem
- **Weeks 32-36:** Tertiary sulci, cortex approaches near-term complexity
- **Weeks 36-40:** Final cortical maturation, myelination advancing rostrally
- **Transient fetal structures:**
  - Subplate zone: largest at ~26-32 weeks, critical for thalamocortical circuit formation, visible on MRI as T1-hyperintense band
  - Germinal matrix: periventricular proliferative zone, involutes by term, vulnerable to hemorrhage (IVH in preterm)

#### 4.5.6 Synaptogenesis, Pruning, and Myelination
- Synaptogenesis onset: from mid-gestation, peaks postnatally (varies by region)
- Synaptic pruning: experience-dependent, microglia-mediated, complement system (C1q, C3)
- Critical periods: visual cortex, language areas, sensorimotor
- Myelination timeline: brainstem and spinal cord first, cortical association areas last (into 3rd decade)
- Myelination sequence visible on MRI: progressive T1 signal increase and T2 signal decrease

### 4.6 Neuroimaging

#### 4.6.1 MRI Physics (see also Applied Sciences > MRI Physics In Depth)
- Nuclear magnetic resonance: spin-1/2 nuclei (hydrogen), Larmor precession
- MRI contrast mechanisms relevant to brain: T1w, T2w, PD, FLAIR, DWI, SWI
- Fetal brain MRI challenges: motion, small size, evolving contrast (water content changes)

#### 4.6.2 Functional MRI (fMRI)
- BOLD (Blood-Oxygen-Level-Dependent) contrast
- Hemodynamic response function (HRF): delay ~5-6 seconds
- Task-based fMRI: block design, event-related design
- Resting-state fMRI (rs-fMRI): default mode network, functional connectivity
- Analysis: GLM, independent component analysis (ICA), seed-based correlation
- Preprocessing: motion correction, slice timing, spatial smoothing, normalization
- Resting-state networks: default mode (DMN), dorsal attention, salience, frontoparietal, sensorimotor
- Fetal fMRI: emerging field, resting-state studies showing prenatal network formation

#### 4.6.3 Diffusion Tensor Imaging (DTI)
- Principle: MRI sensitized to water molecule diffusion using pulsed field gradients
- Diffusion tensor: 3x3 symmetric matrix, 6 independent elements
- Eigenvalues (lambda_1, lambda_2, lambda_3) and eigenvectors
- Derived metrics:
  - **Fractional anisotropy (FA):** 0 (isotropic) to 1 (anisotropic), reflects fiber coherence/myelination
  - **Mean diffusivity (MD):** average diffusion magnitude, reflects cellularity/edema
  - **Axial diffusivity (AD):** lambda_1, diffusion parallel to fibers
  - **Radial diffusivity (RD):** (lambda_2 + lambda_3)/2, sensitive to myelination
- Tractography: deterministic (streamline) vs probabilistic algorithms
- Limitations: crossing fibers (DTI assumes single fiber population per voxel)
- Advanced diffusion models: HARDI, DSI, constrained spherical deconvolution (CSD), NODDI
- **Fetal DTI applications:** tracking white matter development, quantifying maturation (FA increases with gestational age), assessing connectivity

#### 4.6.4 Other Neuroimaging Modalities
- EEG/MEG: high temporal resolution, source localization (inverse problem)
- PET: radiotracer imaging, FDG for metabolism, amyloid/tau tracers
- Near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS): portable, suitable for neonates
- Ultrasound: cranial ultrasound in neonates (open fontanelle), Doppler velocimetry

# IV. Applied Sciences

## 1. Biomedical Engineering

### 1.1 MRI Physics In Depth (CRITICAL — Fetal Brain MRI Research)

> **Note:** This section provides detailed MRI physics relevant to Daniel's work on fetal brain MRI reconstruction and processing pipelines at BCH/HMS.

#### 1.1.1 Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Fundamentals
- Spin-1/2 nuclei: hydrogen (^1H) is primary clinical nucleus (gyromagnetic ratio gamma = 42.58 MHz/T)
- Static magnetic field B0: spins align parallel (low energy) or antiparallel (high energy) to field
- Net magnetization vector M0: Boltzmann distribution determines excess parallel spins
- Larmor frequency: omega_0 = gamma * B0 (at 1.5T: 63.87 MHz, at 3T: 127.74 MHz)
- Radiofrequency excitation: B1 field tips magnetization away from B0; flip angle depends on pulse amplitude and duration

#### 1.1.2 T1 Relaxation (Spin-Lattice / Longitudinal)
- Recovery of longitudinal magnetization Mz toward equilibrium M0
- M_z(t) = M_0(1 - e^{-t/T1})
- T1 depends on molecular tumbling rate (correlation time tau_c)
- Tissue-specific at given field strength: gray matter ~1200 ms, white matter ~800 ms, CSF ~4000 ms at 3T
- T1 increases with field strength
- **Fetal brain T1:** substantially different from adult — high water content makes T1 long; inverts contrast pattern (immature white matter appears darker on T1w than cortex, opposite of adult)
- T1-weighted imaging: short TR, short TE
- Contrast agents (gadolinium): shorten T1 (CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy)

#### 1.1.3 T2 Relaxation (Spin-Spin / Transverse)
- Decay of transverse magnetization Mxy
- M_{xy}(t) = M_{xy}(0) * e^{-t/T2}
- T2 < T1 always; caused by spin-spin interactions, irreversible dephasing
- Tissue-specific: gray matter ~100 ms, white matter ~80 ms, CSF ~2000 ms at 3T
- T2-weighted imaging: long TR, long TE — fluids appear bright
- T2* relaxation: includes field inhomogeneity effects (1/T2* = 1/T2 + 1/T2')
- T2* mapping: used in fMRI (BOLD), iron quantification
- **Fetal brain T2:** T2 of fetal brain tissue is longer than adult due to high water content; T2-weighted sequences are the workhorse of fetal MRI because of excellent contrast between developing structures

#### 1.1.4 Pulse Sequences

##### Spin Echo (SE)
- 90-degree excitation followed by 180-degree refocusing pulse at TE/2
- Refocuses T2' dephasing — gives true T2 contrast
- Slow acquisition (single echo per TR)

##### Fast/Turbo Spin Echo (FSE/TSE)
- Multiple 180-degree refocusing pulses per TR (echo train)
- Echo train length (ETL) determines speedup factor
- Effective TE: central k-space line determines contrast
- T2 blurring at long echo trains

##### HASTE (Half-Fourier Acquisition Single-Shot Turbo Spin Echo)
- **THE primary sequence for fetal brain MRI**
- Single-shot: entire image in one TR (typically <1 second per slice)
- Half-Fourier: acquires slightly more than half of k-space, reconstructs remainder via conjugate symmetry
- Advantages for fetal imaging:
  - Fast acquisition freezes fetal motion (each slice acquired in ~300-500 ms)
  - T2-weighted contrast ideal for fetal brain (high tissue water content)
  - Robust to motion between slices (but individual slices are motion-free)
- Limitations:
  - Low SNR (single shot)
  - SAR constraints at 3T
  - Slice-to-slice motion artifacts (slice stacks are not volumetrically consistent)
  - Blurring from long echo train
- **Clinical protocol:** typically 3+ orthogonal stacks acquired, then computationally combined via slice-to-volume reconstruction (SVR)

##### Gradient Echo (GRE)
- No 180-degree refocusing — uses gradient reversal, gives T2* weighting
- Faster than SE, but susceptibility artifacts
- FLASH, SPGR, MPRAGE subtypes

##### Echo Planar Imaging (EPI)
- Single-shot readout of entire k-space after one excitation
- Used in fMRI (BOLD), DWI/DTI
- Very fast but geometric distortion, Nyquist ghosting, susceptibility artifacts
- Particularly challenging for fetal imaging due to field inhomogeneity at tissue-air interfaces

##### Other Important Sequences
- FLAIR: T2-weighted with CSF suppression (inversion recovery, TI ~2000 ms)
- STIR/SPAIR: fat suppression techniques
- SWI (Susceptibility-Weighted Imaging): venous contrast, microbleeds, iron
- Phase contrast MRI: blood flow velocity measurement
- MR spectroscopy (MRS): metabolite quantification (NAA, choline, creatine, lactate)

#### 1.1.5 k-Space and Image Reconstruction
- k-space: spatial frequency domain representation of MR image
- Relationship: image = 2D inverse Fourier transform of k-space
- k-space properties:
  - Center: contrast, low spatial frequencies (gross anatomy)
  - Periphery: edges, high spatial frequencies (fine detail)
- Filling strategies:
  - Cartesian (line-by-line): standard
  - Radial: motion-robust, streaking artifacts
  - Spiral: efficient, off-resonance artifacts
  - Partial Fourier (half-Fourier): used in HASTE
  - Parallel imaging (GRAPPA, SENSE): undersample k-space, use coil sensitivity maps to reconstruct, acceleration factor R
  - Compressed sensing: exploit sparsity, random undersampling
- **Slice-to-Volume Reconstruction (SVR):**
  - Critical technique for fetal brain MRI
  - Problem: fetal motion between/during slice acquisitions means stacks are inconsistent
  - Approach: acquire multiple orthogonal thick-slice stacks, then computationally register all slices to a common 3D volume
  - Steps: (1) slice-to-volume registration, (2) outlier rejection (corrupted slices), (3) super-resolution reconstruction (combine low-resolution slices into isotropic high-resolution volume)
  - Software: NiftyMIC, SVRTK, IRTK, NeSVoR (deep learning-based)
  - Enables isotropic ~0.5-0.8 mm resolution from 2-3 mm acquired slices

#### 1.1.6 MRI Artifacts
- Motion artifacts: ghosting in phase-encode direction, blurring
  - **Fetal motion:** primary challenge — unpredictable, non-rigid
  - Mitigation: fast single-shot sequences (HASTE), prospective/retrospective motion correction, SVR
- Susceptibility artifacts: signal loss/distortion at air-tissue interfaces
- Chemical shift: fat-water frequency difference causes spatial misregistration
- Aliasing (wraparound): FOV smaller than object
- Truncation (Gibbs ringing): finite sampling of k-space, ringing at sharp edges
- Zipper artifact: RF interference
- B1 inhomogeneity: signal intensity variation, especially at high field (3T)
  - Relevant to fetal imaging: maternal body habitus affects B1 distribution

#### 1.1.7 MRI Safety
- Static field hazards: projectile effect, implant torque
- Gradient field: peripheral nerve stimulation, acoustic noise
- RF field: specific absorption rate (SAR), tissue heating
- **Pregnancy considerations:** no ionizing radiation (advantage over CT), gadolinium contraindicated, 1.5T generally preferred (lower SAR), 3T increasingly used with careful SAR management
- Quench procedures
