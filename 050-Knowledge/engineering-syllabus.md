# Engineering Syllabus

> Applied sciences that design, build, and optimize systems, structures, and processes.

Back to [[academic-taxonomy|Universal Taxonomy]] | [[knowledge-index|Knowledge Index]]

---

## 15. Engineering

### Electrical & Computer Engineering
- [[courses/electrical-engineering|Electrical Engineering]] — Circuit theory (KVL/KCL, Thévenin), AC analysis (impedance, phasors), op-amps, digital logic (Boolean algebra, FSMs), signal processing (Nyquist, DFT, filters), control systems (transfer functions, PID, Bode/Nyquist stability)

### Mechanical Engineering
- [[courses/mechanical-engineering|Mechanical Engineering]] — Statics (free body diagrams, equilibrium), dynamics (Newton/Lagrange), strength of materials ($\sigma = F/A$, $\tau = VQ/It$), thermodynamics applications (cycles: Carnot, Rankine, Otto, Diesel), fluid systems (pipe flow, pumps, turbines), machine design (bearings, gears, shafts), vibrations, FEA

### Civil Engineering
- [[courses/civil-engineering|Civil Engineering]] — Structural analysis (beams, trusses, frames — moment/shear diagrams), reinforced concrete/steel design, geotechnical (soil mechanics, foundations, slope stability), transportation (traffic flow, highway design), water resources (open channel flow, stormwater), construction management

### Chemical Engineering
- [[courses/chemical-engineering|Chemical Engineering]] — Transport phenomena (momentum, heat, mass transfer), reaction engineering (CSTR, PFR, batch: $r_A = -dC_A/dt$), process design (PFDs, P&IDs), separations (distillation, absorption, extraction), process control, thermodynamics (fugacity, activity coefficients, equations of state)

### Aerospace Engineering
- [[courses/aerospace-engineering|Aerospace Engineering]] — Aerodynamics (lift/drag, $C_L, C_D$, thin airfoil theory, compressible flow), propulsion (jet engines: Brayton cycle, rockets: $\Delta v = v_e \ln(m_0/m_f)$), orbital mechanics (Kepler, Hohmann transfers), structural analysis (lightweight materials), flight dynamics and control, space systems

### Biomedical Engineering
- [[courses/biomedical-engineering|Biomedical Engineering]] — Medical imaging (MRI: $S \propto \rho(1-e^{-TR/T_1})e^{-TE/T_2}$, CT, ultrasound, PET), biosignal processing (ECG, EEG, EMG), biomechanics, tissue engineering (scaffolds, bioreactors), neural interfaces, regulatory (FDA), prosthetics and rehabilitation

### Nuclear Engineering
- Nuclear Engineering — Reactor physics (neutron transport, criticality $k_{eff} = 1$, four-factor formula), reactor types (PWR, BWR, CANDU, fast breeders), radiation shielding (attenuation $I = I_0 e^{-\mu x}$), nuclear fuel cycle, fusion energy, radiation protection (dosimetry, ALARA)

### Environmental Engineering
- Environmental Engineering — Water treatment (coagulation, filtration, disinfection), wastewater (activated sludge, BOD/COD), air quality (dispersion modeling, emissions control), solid/hazardous waste, site remediation, environmental impact assessment
