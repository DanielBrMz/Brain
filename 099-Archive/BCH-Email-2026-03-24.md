Hi team,

Please find below the minutes from our March 24 meeting. I've also attached the SURE paper (Pfaff et al., 2023) for reference in relation to item 3.

**1. Brain Mask Conditioning (Run18) — Results**

I presented results from Run18, which replaced the 4-class tissue map FiLM conditioning with a 2-class brain mask. Brain masks are already available in raw stack space, so no inverse transform projection is required.

Run18 trained for 100 epochs on sejong (3× RTX A5000), with best validation loss at epoch 82. Results show a substantial improvement over Run17:

| Metric | Run17 | Run18 |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Best val_sup | 0.0790 | 0.0541 (−31.5%) |
| Blur PSNR gain | +0.32 dB | +4.04 dB |
| Noise PSNR gain | +3.43 dB | +5.10 dB |
| Overall SSIM gain | +0.081 | +0.126 |

The underperformance of Run17 is attributed to the inaccuracy of tissue label projection onto raw stacks. The brain mask approach avoids that problem entirely and establishes a strong new baseline. Visual comparisons were presented including 10 synthetic test pairs across 5 degradation types and a gallery of 24 real low-quality subjects.

**2. NeSVoR Inverse Transform Pipeline — In Progress**

Work is ongoing to accurately project tissue segmentation labels from the reconstructed volume back onto individual raw stack slices for proper tissue-level FiLM conditioning in future runs.

`project_labels.py` applies the per-slice affine matrices from NeSVoR's `--simulated-slices` output using:

```
vol_voxels = inv(vol_affine) @ slice_affine @ slice_voxels
```

Projection was validated visually on FCB080 and the alignment is consistent across subjects. Batch processing is running across all training subjects. As of the meeting, 25+ CHD subjects were complete.

**3. Next Steps**

- Complete batch projection across all remaining protocols (ASD, Placenta, Normative)
- Project actual tissue segmentation labels onto raw slices using the validated pipeline
- Train a new run with correctly projected tissue labels as FiLM conditioning and compare against the Run18 brain mask baseline
- Incorporate a SURE (Stein's Unbiased Risk Estimator) loss term to leverage the 317 real low-quality subjects as a self-supervised denoising signal, complementing the supervised loss on synthetic pairs (see attached paper)
- Significantly expand the training dataset with synthetic images covering the full range of degradation parameter values to achieve complete degradation space coverage
