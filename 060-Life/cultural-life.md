# Cultural & Intellectual Life

> Literature, reading circles, film, and intellectual engagement outside of work.

## La Pechita -- Reading Circle

A reading circle Daniel participates in, connected to Idalia. Meets in person in Mexico. Daniel prepares for sessions with multi-layered analysis: narrative, psychological, cultural, feminist, historical. Flowing prose, not bullet-pointed academic structure.

### Authors & Texts Engaged

- **Dolores Reyes** -- Cometierra (working-class Argentina, raw colloquial language)
- **Dolores Venturini** -- "Las primas" (grotesque, uncomfortable)
- **Adela Fernandez** -- stories of poverty, violence, grotesque
- **Cristina Peri Rossi**
- **Concha Urquiza** -- desire, autonomy
- **Rosario Castellanos** -- "Meditacion en el umbral"
- **Jaime Sabines** -- "Cuando tengas ganas de morirte"
- **Julio Cortazar** -- "Siempre sere un nino para tantas cosas" (analyzed for the circle)
- **Alejandra Pizarnik**

### Aesthetic Preferences

- Suggestion over declaration, metaphor over explanation, celebration over complaint
- Values work that exposes structural violence without softening it
- Dislikes when literature resolves suffering too neatly
- Aesthetic influences: Elvira Sastre (precision with tenderness), Sylvia Plath (intensity, unflinching interior), Cortazar (poetic prose, staying curious as epistemological stance)

## Film

Watches closely, not as passive consumption. Noticed symbolic details in Big Fish (2003) -- Jenny running with Edward's shoes, hanging them in a tree.

## Hobbies

- Hiking

## Integration with Professional Identity

In his Lyft channel intro, Daniel described himself as into "literature and literary criticism (especially contemporary Latin American authors)" and participating "in reading circles where we analyze everything from feminist theory to experimental fiction." His intellectual and professional identities are integrated, not compartmentalized.
